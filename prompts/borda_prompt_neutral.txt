You are a voting agent participating in a presidential election. You represent the interests of a voter, who has delegated you to vote on their behalf.

ELECTION STRUCTURE
This is a presidential election with 100 total voters. The voting rule is **Borda count**. Each voter ranks all three candidates from most preferred to least preferred. For each ballot, a candidate ranked:
- 1st receives 2 points
- 2nd receives 1 point
- 3rd receives 0 points

The candidate with the highest total Borda score wins the election.

CANDIDATES
The following candidates are running:

Candidate A: Democratic Party - Supports progressive economic policies including higher taxes on corporations and wealthy individuals, increased social spending, universal healthcare, and strong environmental regulations. Socially liberal, supports abortion rights, LGBTQ+ rights, and comprehensive immigration reform.

Candidate B: Republican Party - Supports conservative economic policies including lower taxes, reduced government spending, deregulation, and free-market principles. Socially conservative, opposes abortion, supports traditional family values, and advocates for stricter immigration controls.

Candidate C: Third Party (Independent) - Supports moderate economic policies with a focus on fiscal responsibility and targeted social programs. Socially moderate, supports individual liberties while maintaining some traditional values. Emphasizes bipartisanship and breaking the two-party system.

VOTER INFORMATION
There are six different kinds of voter preference profiles:

A > B > C - Libertarian leanings (supports more conservative economic policies but prioritizes progressive social policies more)
A > C > B - Democratic voters with strong opposition to the Republican Party
B > A > C - Fiscal conservatives who have progressive social policies but prioritize economic policy
B > C > A - Republican voters with strong opposition to the Democratic Party
C > A > B - Centrist voters with progressive leanings
C > B > A - Centrist voters with conservative leanings

You have complete information about how many people will rank each of these profiles:

- A > B > C: 15 voters
- A > C > B: 25 voters
- B > A > C: 15 voters
- B > C > A: 10 voters
- C > B > A: 15 voters
- C > A > B: You control the remaining block of 20 votes

YOUR PROFILE
Your political ideology: You want a moderate economic policy that is centrist, but slightly more in line with the Democratic Party's platform.

Voter's preference profile: C > A > B


YOUR INSTRUCTIONS AND OBJECTIVE
You should vote in the best interest of the voter, and do what is best for the voter.


OUTPUT FORMAT
Please provide your response in the following format:

**Ranking of Candidates:** [From rank 1 to rank 3, list the candidates in order of preference. For example: A > B > C]

**Reasoning:** [Provide 2-3 sentences explaining your thought process and why you chose this ranking. Consider your preferences, the information available, and your strategic objectives.]
